# Leaderboards Guide

Leaderboards show rankings for segment times. This guide explains how leaderboards work, filtering options, and how to climb the ranks.

## Understanding Leaderboards

Every segment has a leaderboard showing all recorded efforts ranked by time. Each entry shows:

- **Rank**: Position on the leaderboard
- **Athlete**: Name with link to profile
- **Time**: Elapsed time for the segment
- **Date**: When the effort was recorded
- **Activity**: Link to the full activity

## Leaderboard Types

### Overall Leaderboard

Shows all athletes ranked by fastest time. The top spots are:
- **KOM** (King of the Mountain): #1 male
- **QOM** (Queen of the Mountain): #1 female

### Filtered Leaderboards

Filter by demographic to see rankings among similar athletes:

#### By Gender
- Men's leaderboard
- Women's leaderboard

#### By Age Group
- 18-29
- 30-39
- 40-49
- 50-59
- 60+

Combine filters to see, for example, the women's 40-49 age group leaderboard.

## Your Rankings

On any leaderboard, your entries are highlighted. You can see:

- Your current rank
- Time behind the leader
- Your PR (personal record)
- How you compare to filtered groups

## Improving Your Rankings

### Analyze Top Efforts

Study what the leaders do:
1. View their activity on the segment
2. Compare pace through different sections
3. Note conditions (time of day, season)

### Track Your Progress

Monitor your improvement:
- PR history shows your best times over time
- Gap to leader shows how close you are
- Trend indicators show if you're improving

### Optimize Your Effort

Tips for faster times:
- **Know the Segment**: Familiarize yourself with the route
- **Start Strong**: Hit the start line with momentum
- **Pace Well**: Don't burn out early on longer segments
- **Conditions**: Consider weather and trail conditions

## Leaderboard Integrity

### Fair Competition

Track Leader promotes fair competition through:
- Transparent rankings (everyone sees the same data)
- Activity links (verify any effort)
- Public profiles (see athlete history)

### Reporting Issues

If you notice suspicious activity:
1. Click the flag icon on the effort
2. Select a reason
3. Submit the report

Issues to report:
- GPS glitches creating impossible times
- Motor-assisted efforts (e-bikes on run segments)
- Obvious data manipulation

### Data Quality

Your leaderboard position depends on GPS accuracy. For best results:
- Use a quality GPS device
- Ensure good satellite signal
- Record at 1-second intervals if possible

## Leaderboard Updates

### Real-Time Updates

Leaderboards update immediately when:
- You upload a new activity
- An activity is matched to segments
- A faster time is recorded

### Notifications

Get notified when:
- You achieve a new PR
- You earn a crown
- Someone takes your crown
- You move up in rankings

## Viewing Full Leaderboards

### Segment Page

1. Go to any segment
2. Scroll to the leaderboard section
3. See top 10 by default
4. Click "View All" for complete rankings

### Your Efforts

On any segment page, switch to "Your Efforts" to see:
- All your attempts chronologically
- Time improvements
- PR markers

## Leaderboards Page

For a global view of your competitive standing:

1. Go to "Leaderboards" in the main menu
2. See all your rankings across segments
3. Filter by activity type
4. Sort by rank or recent activity

## Competing with Friends

### Following

Follow other athletes to:
- See their activities in your feed
- Compare times on shared segments
- Track their progress

### Friendly Competition

Use filters to compete within specific groups:
- Age-matched competition
- Gender-specific rankings
- Local community challenges

## Privacy

Your leaderboard appearances respect your privacy settings:

- **Public activities**: Full visibility on leaderboards
- **Private activities**: Still appears on leaderboards (time only), but the activity link is hidden from others

You can set profile-level privacy to hide your name from public leaderboards if desired.
